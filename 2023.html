My Philosophy for the Web

[link—standalone]

I have been angry and frustrated at the abhorrent state of the internet for quite some time now. It has been irritating to see the modern trends in web development steering away from the foundational idea with which the web was created. The internet has now become a playground for big tech corporations rather than being a democratised platform for self-expression. Today most platforms that have today become a "place of ideas" are also controlled by a huge tech corporation. X, Medium and Instagram are cases in point. The web has left its federated nature. At one point all those that wished to have their ideas on "the web", needed to have their own websites which were a private space for self-expression. As Hegel said property is an expression of freedom, of independence. It is also a expression of ones own individuality. That unfortunately finds no place in todays "Web".

There are two parts of this issue that I would like to address. The first is a technical matter that deals with the technologies used in web development today and the other is a more philosophical argument on the centralisation of ideas. The problem with web development today is essentially the fact that it has become extremely maximalist. The time spent on the website today has become a very important metric for those that wish to make monetary gains from their web platforms. This means that the more visually appealing and enchanting the website looks the more our monkey brains are going to spend time looking at it. A very scary trend in this respect is telemetry through cookies which is used by websites to track their users and understand their use patterns. This is then shared with other websites which then allows them to cater their content specifically based on user patters. The recent "analytics" trend is indicative of such change. Most content creators today focus greatly on the ways in which people respond to certain keywords and then strategically use them on their websites to rope in unsuspecting users. This has also had a great effect on the performance of the Web as a information sharing platform. While in the early days the Web was made up of simple HTML pages with text and/or multimedia, the internet was also accessible through simple dial up connections which did not go beyond a few megabytes. Today it is the opposite. The average consumer uses around 1GiB of data per day as per telecom plans here in India. A simple experiment can further help elucidate this point. I invite you to record the time it takes for you web browser to visit the websites of CNN, BBC, The Hindu etc, and the time it takes to load up my website. The difference is shocking. Not only that, Google AdSense is causing a lot of the damage we see today, not only in the sphere of web advertisements but also in the space of web privacy cookies and web telemetry. The ease with which anyone can use Google AdSense to place advertisements on their website and use it to monetise their platform has caused great issues in the way the Web works today. It has made most website unbearable with the abominable amount of ads that websites serve to their users today1. The focus on making the web simpler is also an attempt to make the web a more quick and efficient source of information.

On the philosophical question I will speak less. I am shocked at the way in which we are ready to part with our individual autonomy, which also means our autonomy as a society, while being a part of a centralised system of ideas. Today as a society we must tread with caution. Physical places which facilitate interaction, exchange of ideas and public discourse are way more difficult to censor. Habermas talks about how the cafe’s in Paris became an important centre of political engagement. They became small microcosms that engaged with the contemporary issues of their time in their own way and were in no compulsion from any other agent. The power of such public engagement is being undermined today. With reports of censorship on various platforms because of “Terms of Service” violations are alarming when used for political ends. I donot blame those in power that use it, mostly because we as a community have given them that power. Today social engagement is in reality so fragile that today that a power outage can dismantle and disrupt the entire house of cards. This is the reality that I am afraid of, and this website is my own way of fighting such oppression
